overflow

Overflow is a CSS property that controls what happens when content overflows its container. It can take values like visible, hidden, scroll, and auto.

Overflow: visible; - The content is not clipped and will overflow the box.

Overflow: hidden; - The content is clipped and will not be visible outside the box.

Overflow: scroll; - The content is clipped, but a scrollbar is added to allow scrolling.

Overflow: auto; - The content is clipped, and a scrollbar is added only if necessary.

Overflow: inherit; - The property inherits the value from its parent element.

Overflow: initial; - The property is set to its default value.

Overflow: unset; - The property is reset to its natural value.

Overflow: revert; - The property is reset to the value it would have had if the stylesheet had not changed it.

float

Float is a CSS property that allows elements to be positioned to the left or right of their container, allowing text and inline elements to wrap around them.

Float: left; - The element floats to the left of its container.

Float: right; - The element floats to the right of its container.

Float: none; - The element does not float and will be displayed in the normal flow of the document.
